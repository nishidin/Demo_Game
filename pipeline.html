The learning design question — why this matters to get right
What the research says
Cultural adjustment is not just about knowledge — it is about behaviour rehearsal. Kolb's experiential learning cycle shows that people retain and act on learning when they experience a situation, reflect on it, conceptualise the rule, and then experiment again. This is exactly the game loop: encounter → fail or succeed → cultural explanation → retry. The game is designed as applied learning theory, not just entertainment.
What needs to be answered by Month 3
How do we define and measure "cultural readiness"? What does progression actually look like across sessions? How do we ensure the scenarios are proximal — challenging enough to stretch the player but not so foreign they disengage? These are the pedagogical questions that will determine whether this is a serious educational tool or just a quiz. The framework document produced in Month 3 is the answer to all of them.
